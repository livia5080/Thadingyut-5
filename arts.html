Get creative this Thadingyut with DIY decoration ideas!

Using simple materials like paper, yarn, and recycled plastic, you can craft beautiful, eco-friendly pieces to brighten your home and celebrate the Festival of Lights. From colorful lanterns to unique handmade ornaments, our easy tutorials will help you add a personal, festive touch to your celebrations!

Safety rules!

Use electric candles: Opt for battery-operated LED candles or lights inside paper lanterns instead of real flames to avoid fire hazards.

Keep away from heat sources: Ensure your paper decorations are placed away from any heat sources like light bulbs or heaters.

Secure hanging items: Make sure all hanging decorations, such as lanterns, are properly secured to prevent them from falling.

Avoid flammable objects: Keep paper lanterns and decorations away from curtains, furniture, and other flammable materials.

Choose non-toxic materials: Use non-toxic paints, glues, and adhesives, especially when children are involved in the crafting process.

Safety rules!

Use LED lights instead of real candles: If incorporating lighting, opt for battery-operated LED lights to avoid fire hazards.

Keep yarn decorations away from open flames: Yarn is highly flammable, so make sure to place your decorations far from candles, stoves, or any heat sources.

Secure hanging yarn items: Ensure that yarn decorations are securely fastened, especially if they're used for hanging, to prevent them from falling and becoming tripping hazards.

Avoid excessive tension: When stringing yarn decorations, don’t pull the yarn too tightly to prevent it from snapping and causing injury.

Keep yarn away from small children and pets: Loose yarn can pose a choking hazard to small children and pets. Ensure all decorations are out of their reach.

Safety rules!

Use LED lights: If your plastic decorations require lighting, choose battery-operated LED lights, as they generate less heat and are safer around plastic materials.

Keep plastic decorations away from heat: Plastics can melt or catch fire when exposed to high heat or open flames, so avoid placing them near candles, stoves, or heaters.

Secure hanging decorations: Ensure that plastic decorations, especially those hung up, are fastened securely to prevent them from falling and causing injury.

Avoid sharp edges: When cutting or crafting plastic decorations, smooth out any sharp edges to prevent cuts or scratches.

Choose non-toxic materials: Use non-toxic paints, glues, and adhesives, especially when children are involved in the crafting process.
