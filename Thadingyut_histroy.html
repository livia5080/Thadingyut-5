All about Thadingyut

Thadingyut, also known as the Festival of Lights, is an important celebration in Myanmar that marks the end of the Buddhist Lent. It usually takes place in October during the full moon of the month called Thadingyut. This festival lasts for three days and honors the Buddha's return from heaven after teaching his mother about Buddhism.

What does we do during Thadingyut?

During Thadingyut, people decorate their homes and streets with colorful lights and candles, symbolizing the light of knowledge and enlightenment. Many enjoy cultural activities like traditional music and dance performances called Zat Pwes and also many people visit pagodas to offer food to monks and light candles as a way to show respect and gratitude.
